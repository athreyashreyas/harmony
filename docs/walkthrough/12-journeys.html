Chapter 12

User Journeys

The end-to-end paths a real person takes, and what happens under each tap. If you can narrate these, you can demo the app from memory.

1 · Onboarding

  1. Sign up → the first name is stashed in localStorage as a pending value (so a confirmation round-trip never loses it).
  2. Name your areas (3–20), pick colours from the family-grouped picker, add a starter habit per area, and write the "why" sentence for each — with a live preview of how a future reminder would read.
  3. The whole draft lives in localStorage, so a reload mid-flow resumes exactly where you were.
  4. On finish, everything commits in one Dexie transaction (areas + habits together), and you land on the guide (the intended first stop), with lastSeenVersion set so "What's new" won't greet you immediately.

2 · A day of use (Home)

  • Tap a circle → an optimistic Log is written to Dexie and the store, so the check, the count, and the Bloom redraw before any network call; the outbox mirrors it up.
  • Tap again to undo (a soft-delete that syncs).
  • Press & hold a habit → leave a "how did that feel?" note (which also marks the day tended).
  • Tap an area chip (or a Bloom petal) → filter the day to that area; hold a chip → edit the area in place.
  • Sort control → order the list by time of day (default; anytime last), priority, or "still to do"; the choice syncs across devices and the list animates into its new order.
  • Tugs section → one tap to note a tug for today; it shows "days since the last one" and quietly steps aside after a while.
  • Rituals section → Begin a flow (ch. 09).

3 · Editing the past (Log)

The Log is a calm month calendar of what you tended, with your notes. Tap any past day to set the record straight — mark something you forgot, or unmark a mistaken tap. Thanks to the watermark sync (ch. 06), even an edit to a months-old day propagates to your other devices immediately on their next sync.

4 · Across devices

  • Sign in on a new phone/tablet → a full seed pulls your whole history; everything is just there.
  • From then on, changes flow instantly via Realtime when both devices are online, and via the watermark catch-up the moment an offline device reconnects.
  • The status dot (top-right) shows offline / syncing / up-to-date; tapping it opens a Sync popup to sync on demand or pick up a new app version.

5 · Reminders arriving

Turn on reminders per device (on iPhone, add to home screen first). Give a habit a time → the worker sends a soft nudge at that minute on days it's due, landing within ~a minute. Quiet hours suppress the app's own nudges (drift, the evening round-up) but not your chosen reminder times. When an area you said mattered goes quiet, your own sentence comes back — on Home as a banner and as a push.

6 · The guide & "What's new"

"How Harmony works" has two sides: What's new (the latest release in focus, full version history below, each with a little illustration of the feature it brought) and an illustrated walk-through of everything. It's shown once after onboarding and reachable any time under Me. On a version bump, "What's new" greets you exactly once per account (synced), then steps aside.

7 · Account management

  • Forgot password → email link → set a new one on the reset screen.
  • Delete account → the worker removes all your data and your auth user (a true deletion), then the device wipes local data.
  • Sign out → flush the queue while still authenticated, then wipe Dexie so nothing is left for the next person.
The thread through all of theseEvery journey is instant and offline-safe because writes hit Dexie first, and consistent across devices because the sync layer catches up completely and cheaply. The user never waits on, or is blocked by, the network.
