Press Release & FAQ

The press release

Habits, but quieter.

Most habit apps are scoreboards. They count days in a row, flash red when you slip, and quietly make you feel worse on the days you most needed something gentle. Harmony takes the opposite view. It begins not with a checklist but with a question: what parts of your life actually matter to you?

During a short, unhurried onboarding, you name your own areas of life, write in your own words why each one matters, and put them in the order that feels true. Then you add one small habit for each. From that day on, Harmony's home screen is a living Bloom: a ring of petals, one per area, that open as you tend to them and recede softly when life pulls you away. There are no streaks to break and nothing to lose.

"We wanted the opposite of a guilt machine. Open it on a hard day and it should feel like a friend who remembers what you care about, not a coach with a stopwatch."

Noor's App Dreamland Ltd., on why Harmony exists

When an area goes quiet, Harmony does something unusual: instead of a generic "you missed your goal," it brings back the sentence you wrote about why that area mattered, in your own words. A gentle line on the home screen, and, if you like, a soft notification at a time you choose. The reminders read like a kind note, never a demand.

Everything you make follows you. Sign in on a phone, a tablet, the web, and your areas, habits, and history are simply there, updating across devices the moment you change them. It works offline, installs to your home screen, and keeps your data private: no ads, no selling, nothing to index.

The heart of it

How the Bloom works

The Bloom is the picture of your last two weeks. Each area of life is one petal. A petal grows outward as you tend to that area and eases back inward as time passes without it. It is a rhythm, not a grade.

Each petal is one area. How far it reaches is how fully you have tended it lately. The two dotted rings are gentle markers, at a third and two thirds of the way out.

A petal at full reach is an area you have kept up with. A short petal is one that is waiting. The center holds your h.

Does it reset every week? Does it drop after a day?

Neither, and this is the part people ask about most. The Bloom uses a rolling 14 day window. There is no Monday reset and no overnight punishment. Every day, the window quietly slides: what you did today counts, and what you did fifteen days ago gently ages out. So a petal does not crash because you missed yesterday; it simply reflects the last two weeks as a whole, recalculated each day.

How a petal decides its reach

For each area, Harmony looks at how many times you tended its habits in the last 14 days, compared with how many times those habits asked to be tended. A habit set to "every day" expects fourteen; "three times a week" expects six; "weekdays" expects ten. Your real completions divided by that expectation gives a ratio.

That ratio is then shaped so that doing exactly what you set out to do reaches about two thirds of the way out (right around the outer dotted ring), and going above and beyond can push a petal to full. This is deliberate: keeping your own cadence should feel good and look healthy, without demanding perfection to ever look "complete."

It grows

Log a habit and its area's petal springs outward within the same moment, before anything touches the network.

It eases back

Go quiet and the petal recedes slowly over days as completions age out of the 14 day window. Never a sudden drop.

It rests, it does not fail

An area with no habits yet, or a brand new one, simply sits as a small bud. That is a calm rest state, not a red mark.

The line under the Bloom

Below the petals, one short caption reads the whole picture. When your areas are broadly in step it says "In a good rhythm today." When one has gone quiet it says "A few areas waiting for you." Otherwise it simply offers "Tend to yourself today." It is a mood, not a metric.

And the rest of the app reuses the same idea

The Insights screen shows the same calculation over a longer 30 day window as soft balance bars, so you can see the shape of a month. Same gentle math, wider lens.

Questions, answered kindly

FAQ

Are there streaks?

No. On purpose. There is nothing to break and nothing to lose. The Bloom shows your last two weeks as a living shape, recalculated daily, so a single missed day never undoes your progress.

If I miss a day, does my Bloom collapse?

No. Because the Bloom reads a rolling 14 day window, one missed day barely moves a petal. Petals recede slowly as older completions age out, never in a punishing drop.

Does the Bloom reset weekly or daily?

It does not reset at all. There is no Monday wipe and no nightly reset. The 14 day window simply slides forward each day: today's tending is added, the day from two weeks ago falls away.

What makes a petal full?

Tending an area's habits as often as they ask, across the last two weeks, brings a petal to about two thirds (the outer dotted ring). Doing more than your set cadence can push it to full. You never need to be perfect for it to look healthy.

How do reminders work, and are they pushy?

They are quiet by design. You can give a habit a reminder time and Harmony will send a soft, gently worded nudge on the days it is due, if it is still unlogged. There is an optional evening round up of anything still waiting. When an area has gone quiet for a while, a reminder may bring back your own words about why it mattered. You control quiet hours, can mute any area, and can turn it all off.

On iPhone, notifications only work once Harmony is added to the home screen. That is an Apple rule, not ours.

Can I schedule a habit for specific days, or for a limited time?

Yes. A habit can be every day, weekdays, weekends, certain days you pick, a number of times a week, or every few days. You can also set a start date and an optional end date, so a habit can run indefinitely or just for a season.

Does my data sync across devices?

Yes. Sign in anywhere and your areas, habits, and history arrive on their own, and stay in step the moment you make a change, thanks to live syncing. It also works offline and catches up when you reconnect.

Is it private? Do you sell my data or show ads?

No ads, ever, and we do not sell your data or use it to profile you. Your information is tied only to your account, stored to sync across your devices, and you can delete your account and its data from Settings at any time.

What if I forget my password?

Use "Forgot your password?" on the sign in screen. You will get an email link that takes you to a page to set a new password, then you sign back in.

Do I need an account? Can I use it on the web?

Yes to an account, so your data can follow you. Harmony runs in the browser, installs as an app on phones and tablets, and works the same across all of them.

For the team

Internal FAQ

Why a rolling window instead of a streak or weekly target?

Streaks punish absence and turn one slip into a quit. A rolling 14 day window makes the product forgiving by construction: the worst a single bad day can do is age in slowly and age out slowly. It also gives a smooth, animatable value for the petals rather than a binary done or not.

Why does hitting cadence read as roughly two thirds, not full?

If meeting your own plan filled a petal completely, there would be nowhere to go and "complete" would invite all-or-nothing thinking. Landing keeping-your-cadence around the outer ring leaves visible headroom for extra effort while making a normal week look healthy and calm. The exact shaping is a tuned constant and is a fair candidate to revisit against real usage.

What are the open numbers we should tune with real data?

The cadence-to-fill shaping, the caption thresholds (broadly in rhythm versus an area being quiet), the two reference ring positions, and the drift sensitivity that decides when an area is "quiet" enough to surface a nudge. All are deliberately conservative defaults today.

How does syncing stay consistent across devices?

Writes save locally first and mirror up immediately. On sign in and on returning to a device, the app pulls and reconciles so deletes and un-logs propagate, not just additions, with a short grace window protecting very recent local writes. Live updates arrive over a realtime channel scoped to the signed in user. Backstops cover dropped sockets. The known gap is durable offline retry, which is the next foundational step.

Why is the push worker deployed separately from the app?

The web app is hosted on Cloudflare Pages, which auto-deploys from the git repository on every push. The notification engine is a separate Cloudflare Worker (it runs on a schedule and sends web push), and it is not wired into that same git auto-deploy, so it ships with its own command. They are two different runtimes with two different deploy paths.
