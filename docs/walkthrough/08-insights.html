Chapter 08

Insights & the Reflection

A one-stop, dynamic read on how you're living — over a selectable range or focused on one area — powered by a pure analytics engine, bespoke dependency-free charts, a Bloom garden of your past weeks, and a warm reflection that never repeats itself.

The analytics engine (lib/insights/analytics.ts)

computeInsights({areas, habits, logs}, range, {focusAreaId}) is pure and deterministic given now. Everything on the screen derives from its single output, so every chart tells a consistent story.

FieldWhat it is
rangeweek | month | year | all — rescopes every chart at once.
focusAreaIdScope the whole page to one area (a drill-down).
trendPer-bucket adherence ratio (daily for week/month, monthly for year/all).
trendDelta / prevTendsMomentum vs the previous equal period.
calendarDaily cells for the whole calendar year, including future days (drawn faint) so the grid reads complete.
weekday[7] / segments[4]Rhythm: which days & times of day you show up most.
runsCurrent & longest consecutive days shown up (gentle, celebrate-only).

tugs[] / tugStats[]Lift-vs-drag series + per-tug counts and days-since.
summaryTends, days shown up, active areas/habits, top area.

Bespoke charts — no dependency

Every visualization is hand-built SVG/CSS (components/charts/), deliberately not a charting library — it keeps the bundle small (kind to the free-tier egress) and lets each chart wear the theme:

  • TrendChart — an area+line of momentum, stroke kept even under stretch via vector-effect: non-scaling-stroke.
  • Bars / HBars — weekday bars and horizontal time-of-day bars (full labels: Morning/Afternoon/Evening/Night).
  • CalendarHeatmap — a contribution grid that opens anchored on the present (auto-scrolls so the current month is in view), with month labels across the top (spaced so they never crowd) and all seven weekday labels down the left; today is ringed; future days are dashed outlines. Never red.
  • RadarChart — a "balance constellation" of your areas, a companion to the Bloom showing whether attention is spread or gathered.
  • Sparkline / DivergingBars — per-habit rhythm; tugs rising above / pulling below the line.

The Bloom garden

A second tab in Insights: every past week, pressed and kept as its own mini Bloom, scrollable back through the seasons. It's computed live (computeGarden) from your logs — nothing extra is stored. Each week re-blooms by calling the very same computeAreaActivity with that week's endISO and a 7-day window (this is why the Bloom function took an endISO parameter). Bounded to 78 weeks, and made fast by pre-grouping logs by area so each week scans only that area's logs.

Correctness noteInsights and the garden read the full log history from Dexie (a local, zero-egress read), not the 60-day UI window — otherwise Year/All ranges and older garden weeks would silently under-report. This was a real bug caught and fixed in the security/perf audit (ch. 14).

The reflection — a voice that never repeats

Insights closes with a warm, human paragraph (lib/insights/reflection.ts). The brief was exacting: informative, motivational, accurate, human, empathetic, joyful, kind — and it must not feel repetitive over days, weeks, months, years, while staying stable (no flicker) for a given state.

How it achieves both

  1. It rotates which truths it surfaces. Each period there are many applicable observations (momentum, rhythm, runs, breadth, a standout habit, a quiet area held kindly using your own "why", tugs, weekday leanings). A seed picks a fresh couple to feature — so different periods highlight different things, not just different words.
  2. It rotates how each is said — large phrasing pools per slot.
  3. The seed is a hash of the period (the range's start) plus your actual numbers. Same state → identical text (no flicker on re-open). New day/new numbers → fresh wording. Fully deterministic, no randomness.
seed = hash( from · range · tends · daysShownUp · weekday[] · bestSegment · runs · Δmomentum · per-area ratios )
featured = the applicable observations, sorted by hash(seed, id), take 2

The voice rules (hard-won)

  • No first person. "from where I'm sitting" was removed — the app isn't a character.
  • No eggshells. "No pressure at all, but…" became confident nudges ("give it a small hello this week"). It doesn't apologise for suggesting what you already said you wanted.
  • No streaks-as-shame. Runs are celebrated, never used to guilt. A quiet area is held kindly, quoting your own reason back.
  • It always closes on a genuine pat on the back.

A focused (single-area) reflection is a separate, similarly-structured generator. Both are unit-tested for determinism, for reflecting real counts, and for reading differently across periods.

Decision · rules-based now, LLM laterThe reflection is deliberately rules-based (no model call) so it's instant, free, private, and deterministic. It's structured so LLM processing can be layered on later — the intent was always to make it feel "almost LLM-generated" first, then optionally add real generation.
